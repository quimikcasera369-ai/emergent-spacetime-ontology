# Ontological Spacetime Hypothesis

## Idea

Modern physics, especially General Relativity, describes the geometry of spacetime and how it interacts with matter and energy. However, it does not necessarily explain the ontological origin of spacetime itself.

This project explores the hypothesis that spacetime is not fundamental, but an emergent structure arising from a deeper ontological layer.

## Conceptual Structure

The model proposes a chain of emergence:

Ω → R → E → Φ → Ψ → Gμν

Where:

Ω : fundamental ontology  
R : relational structure emerging from Ω  
E : emergence operator that organizes relations  
Φ : formation of spacetime structure  
Ψ : transformation into observable geometric properties  
Gμν : relativistic spacetime geometry

## Conceptual Equation

$$
G_{\mu\nu} = \Psi(\Phi(\mathcal{E}(R(\Omega))))
$$

## Interpretation

This equation expresses the idea that relativistic spacetime geometry may be the final emergent layer of a deeper ontological process.

In this view:

- Relativity describes the geometry of spacetime.
- Ontology seeks the underlying structure that produces it.
